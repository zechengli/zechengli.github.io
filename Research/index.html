Research

I’ve had three meaningful research experiences so far, and I deeply appreciate all the professors who have guided me along the way.

Undergraduate Research with Dr. Sheng Wei (2016–2017)

During my undergraduate studies, I worked with Dr. Sheng Wei on digital signatures and potential vulnerabilities in Ring doorbells. Although we didn’t publish a paper, I gained valuable insights into hardware and multimedia security.

Later, I participated in the DAC 2018 System Design Contest, where I trained a machine learning model for image recognition — my first exposure to ML research.

Archived student page

Master’s Research with Dr. Shuai Nie (2023–2024)

With Dr. Shuai Nie, I modeled a wireless two-ray propagation system to optimize Wi-Fi access point placement in complex environments using a depth camera. This hands-on research deepened my understanding of RF propagation.

Archived group page

Research with Dr. Shubhendu Bhardwaj (2024–Present)

Working with Dr. Bhardwaj, I co-authored a paper (to appear at IEEE-APS 2025) on a wireless temperature sensing system using impedance shifts caused by a bimetallic strip. I led the design and measurement aspects.

Looking Ahead

I’m interested in pursuing a Ph.D. at the intersection of wireless communication and human health. I want to build wearable devices or systems that enhance well-being and performance — combining engineering with purpose.

I believe research shouldn’t just be about publishing papers — it should be about building things that truly benefit the world. I admire professors who genuinely enjoy talking about their research, even when it involves hardship and setbacks. That passion shows me that research is a stage for warriors — those who endure and shine through challenges.
