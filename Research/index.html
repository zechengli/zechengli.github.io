Research

I’ve had three meaningful research experiences so far, and I deeply appreciate all the professors who guided me along the way.

Undergraduate Research with Dr. Sheng Wei (2017–2018)

During my undergraduate studies, I worked with Dr. Sheng Wei, focusing on digital signatures — signing files with a private key and verifying with a public key. Our research explored vulnerabilities in Ring doorbells, especially in video signature verification. Though we didn’t publish, it was a foundational experience for me.

I also participated in the DAC 2018 System Design Contest, training my first machine learning model for image recognition. It didn’t win, but it sparked my interest in ML.

Master's Research with Dr. Shuai Nie (2023–2024)

I joined Dr. Shuai Nie’s lab to secure funding as a master’s student, and it turned into an eye-opening experience. I built a two-ray wireless propagation model for optimizing Wi-Fi placement using depth cameras. It was my first deep dive into wireless communication and ray tracing.

Although we didn’t publish, I developed strong technical skills and a clearer sense of academic research expectations.

Research with Dr. Shubhendu Bhardwaj (2024–Now)

Under Dr. Shubhendu Bhardwaj, I worked on a wireless temperature sensing system using bimetallic strips and impedance reflection. Changes in temperature affect the S11 parameter, which we monitor for fault prediction in turbines and trains. I handled antenna design, RF measurements, and led our paper submission to IEEE-APS 2025.

Dr. Bhardwaj has been an incredible mentor — supportive, patient, and deeply knowledgeable. This was the first time I truly felt like a contributing researcher.

Looking Ahead — Ph.D. Aspirations

If I pursue a Ph.D., I’d love to work on wearable devices or human-performance-centered engineering. My interests lie at the intersection of health and wireless systems — improving people’s lives through smart tech.

I’m also drawn to passionate mentors who truly care about innovation beyond papers and funding. While I may not be the strongest on paper, I bring grit, curiosity, and persistence to every opportunity.
